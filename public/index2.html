Over 25% of Trade

Over 25% of Trade Flows Through West Coast Ports

Over a quarter America’s container trade occurs at West Coast ports. These ships are primarily engaged in trade with Asia and carry electronics, apparel, automobile parts, furniture, scrap metals, and other manufactured goods.

Largest Container Ships

West Coast Ships Have the Largest Container Ships

However, West Coast ports receive larger ships than other regions of the country. The average container vessel calling in West Coast ports carries almost 1,000 more containers than those visiting the East Coast. Ships anchored off the West Coast right now are carrying far more cargo than the average cargo ship, and almost 3 times what cargo ships visiting the Gulf Coast and Caribbean are carrying.

These Large Ships Have Fueled the West Coast's Growth

Fueled by business from these larger cargo ships, combined with growing Trans-Pacific trade, the value of goods flowing through West Coast ports has increased 85% over the last ten years, accounting for almost 30% of the national total.
